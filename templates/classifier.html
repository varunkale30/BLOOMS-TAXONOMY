üéì Bloom's Taxonomy Reference Guide

Understanding cognitive complexity levels for effective educational assessment

1. Remember

üß†

Recall facts and basic concepts from memory

Key Verbs: define, list, identify, recall, state, name, describe, tell
Example Questions:
  • "What is photosynthesis?"
  • "List the planets in our solar system"
  • "Define democracy"
Purpose: Building foundational knowledge base

2. Understand

üí°

Explain ideas and concepts in your own words

Key Verbs: explain, describe, interpret, summarize, paraphrase, discuss
Example Questions:
  • "Explain how photosynthesis works"
  • "Summarize the main causes of World War II"
  • "What does this poem mean?"
Purpose: Demonstrating comprehension and meaning-making

3. Apply

‚ö°

Use information in new situations and contexts

Key Verbs: apply, use, solve, demonstrate, calculate, implement, execute
Example Questions:
  • "Calculate the area of this triangle"
  • "Apply Newton's laws to solve this problem"
  • "Use this formula to find the solution"
Purpose: Transferring knowledge to practical situations

4. Analyze

üîç

Break down information and examine relationships

Key Verbs: analyze, compare, contrast, examine, investigate, categorize
Example Questions:
  • "Compare and contrast democracy vs. autocracy"
  • "Analyze the causes of climate change"
  • "What are the key differences between these theories?"
Purpose: Developing critical thinking and pattern recognition

5. Evaluate

‚öñÔ∏è

Make judgments and justify decisions with evidence

Key Verbs: evaluate, assess, judge, critique, justify, argue, defend
Example Questions:
  • "Evaluate the effectiveness of this policy"
  • "Which solution is better and why?"
  • "Critique this research methodology"
Purpose: Building judgment skills and evidence-based reasoning

6. Create

üé®

Produce new or original work by combining elements

Key Verbs: create, design, develop, build, compose, generate, invent
Example Questions:
  • "Design a solution for reducing plastic waste"
  • "Create a business plan for a startup"
  • "Develop a new theory to explain this phenomenon"
Purpose: Fostering innovation and original thinking

üí° Pro Tips for Educators

üéØ Balanced Assessment

Aim for a mix of cognitive levels in your assessments. A good distribution might be 20% Remember, 30% Understand, 25% Apply, 15% Analyze, 7% Evaluate, and 3% Create.

üìà Progressive Complexity

Start with lower-order thinking skills and gradually build up to higher-order skills. Each level builds upon the previous ones.
